IE treats persistent systems as resource-constrained processors subject to thermodynamic, information-theoretic, and stability constraints. These constraints arise from the fundamental \textbf{Persistence Principle} to minimize entropic cost (metabolic burden) while maintaining structural identity against environmental entropy.

We hypothesize that the vacuum itself is such a persistent system: a finite-capacity information-processing substrate governed by Shannon-entropy limits. If this hypothesis is correct, the fundamental constants should not be arbitrary tuning parameters, but necessary boundary conditions determined by capacity constraints and the Persistence Principle.

To validate this hypothesis, we utilize the Standard Model not as a paradigm to be replaced, but as a ground-truth dataset for verification. This framework serves as the Geometric Initialization of the Standard Model: while standard Quantum Field Theory treats couplings as inputs, we derive them as the unique persistent solutions to the load of information propagation. The challenge is definitive: to derive the fundamental parameters solely from geometric capacity constraints, without empirical tuning.
